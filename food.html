Jakarta's Traditional Food


Jakarta's culinary scene combines a rich tapestry of herbs, spices, and cooking techniques. From street vendors to upscale restaurants, Jakarta's culinary landscape invites to embark on a flavorful journey, where every meal tells a story of tradition and innovation.







Nasi Uduk


"Nasi Uduk" is a traditional Jakartarian dish (originally from betawi culture) and is now very popular in all of Indonesia. It consists of fragrant coconut rice cooked with coconut milk and various aromatic spices, such as lemongrass, pandan leaves, and galangal. This dish is often served with a variety of side dishes, such as fried chicken, fried shallots, toasted coconut flakes, fried anchovies, and a spicy chili sambal.

Es Selendang Mayang


"Es Selendang Mayang" is a traditional Jakartarian dessert. Popular not only in Jakarta, but also other parts of Java. This sweet and colorful dessert consists of layers of agar-agar (a jelly-like substance made from seaweed) in various vibrant colors, typically red, green, and white, which are cut into small, diamond-shaped pieces. Eventhough not visually appealing but also offers a delightful combination of sweet and refreshing flavors, making it a favorite treat during festive occasions and celebrations.

Asinan Betawi


"Asinan Betawi" is a popular dish originating from Jakarta, specifically the Betawi culture. It is a type of preserved or pickled salad that typically includes a variety of vegetables and fruits, such as cucumber, carrot, cabbage, pineapple, and bean sprouts. The ingredients are mixed together and then generously dressed with a sweet and tangy tamarind-based sauce, often garnished with peanuts and krupuk (deep-fried shrimp crackers).
